Novel Synaptic Weight Programming for Passive Memristor Arrays
Researchers have recently presented, in an innovative Nano Energy paper, a simple but powerful way to program passive memristor crossbar arrays, key building blocks for brain-inspired AI, so they compute more accurately with far less energy. A reset-dominant synaptic weight programming strategy for passive memristor crossbar arrays for high-precision and energy-efficient neuromorphic computing. They validated their approach via hardware-based inference using a spiking neural network trained on the MNIST dataset, suppressing cell-to-cell interference with a mean error of 419.8 nA; 99% of weights stayed within a tight 1.5 µA window. This next-generation technology represents a scalable and power-efficient solution for accurate vector-matrix multiplication in passive memristor arrays.

Configural Processing for Object Recognition in Neural Networks
Why do our brains rely on the spatial relationships between features rather than on the features themselves when recognizing highly familiar objects such as faces? A new Communications Biology research paper explores this question by testing the hypothesis that configural processing drives robust object recognition under varying viewing conditions. The researchers compared neural networks trained to use either configural or local cues in identification tasks with composite letter stimuli, finding that those relying on configural cues generalized more effectively across rotation and scaling in a purely feedforward manner. The same principle extended to naturalistic face images, highlighting the key role of configural processing in robust face recognition.

SAGCRN Encoder-Decoder Framework for Enhanced Traffic Forecasting
A new encoder-decoder framework—Sequence-aware Adaptive Graph Convolutional Recurrent Networks (SAGCRN)—is expected to improve traffic forecasting. The technology presented in Knowledge-Based Systems employs the encoder to augment an input sequence via spatio-temporal contexts and traffic pattern storage. Subsequently, the decoder adaptively learns a new graph structure for prediction. Notably, the framework also optimizes the stored traffic patterns for discrimination. SAGCRN exhibits outstanding performance on three real-world benchmark datasets compared to state-of-the-art alternatives.

Activity-in-the-Loop Kinematics Estimator for 3D Human Kinematics Estimation
In a new Nature Communications research article, scientists have developed an Activity-in-the-loop Kinematics Estimator (AIL-KE), a deep learning model that accurately estimates human kinematics from wearable sensors. The model integrates activity classification into the estimation process, allowing it to leverage behavioral constraints inherent in human motion. Built on the principle that human movement, though highly dimensional, follows limited and activity-specific patterns, AIL-KE achieves precise motion tracking using two inertial measurement units worn on the wrist and chest. The proposed approach achieved low trajectory and shoulder joint angle errors, demonstrating accurate motion tracking with minimal, portable, and cost-effective equipment.

DIOR-ViT for Cancer Classification in Pathology Images
In a new paper published in Medical Image Analysis, scientists have proposed a differential ordinal learning Vision Transformer that simultaneously performs categorical and differential ordinal classification for cancer grading in pathology images. The evaluation on three different cancer datasets shows that the proposed technology can enhance cancer grading accuracy and reliability, outperforming traditional approaches. It is expected to have applications in other diseases and problems that involve an ordinal relationship among class labels.

GLOGCN Technology for Epidemic Prediction
In a breakthrough Knowledge-Based Systems study, scientists propose global temporal feature-based graph convolutional network (GLOGCN) to predict confirmed cases in the spread of infectious diseases. They validated their model using the United States COVID-19, Hungary chickenpox, and German tuberculosis epidemic datasets, finding that GLOGCN outperforms baseline models in terms of low error as well as high robustness. Therefore, this novel technology is expected to provide supplemental data to enhance epidemic policymaking.
